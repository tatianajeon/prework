Hello!

Hi everyone, my name is Tatiana and this is my first html project with Coding Temple! Joining a coding bootcamp is just about the biggest plot twist of my life so far. Looking back, I can identify the times when I was interested in code, such as during the y2k blogging era (xanga, myspace, Wordpress, etc); however, I never thought to make it a career. After some years in the field of dietetics, I find myself wanting to challenge my brain in a different way. My husband kept joking about how he should have gone into coding instead of medicine, and something internalized for me. After talking to a handful of people (friends who code, women who code), I decided to go for it. Though I have a few ideas at the moment, I’m not 100% sure what my career would look like at this point; however, as I learn more I’m gaining more ideas. Not going to lie, sometimes I’m a little nervous because the switch feels so left field: usually RDs go on to start their own private practice or study to become PAs (physician’s assistants), but I don’t know anyone who’s gone into coding. These are the times I just tell myself that I can do it. I’m excited to learn and to get to know my instructors and cohort.

RD life

I’ve worked in various settings in the field of Dietetics. I served as an inpatient RD in the hospital setting covering a variety of unites such as: med-surge, labor and delivery, cardiac, neurological, medical, and surgical ICUs, transplant, and physical rehabilitation. I’ve worked with inpatient psych/mental health facilities (both private and state institutions), and with outpatient settings such as cardiac rehab, oncology, eating disorders partial hospitalization programs, depression/anxiety/chemical dependency programs, long term care, and adult daycare/geriatric care.

My philosophy on nutrition is that it is something that should be intuitive; however, there are a lot of factors that might offset the intuition: mood, emotions, body image, bad experiences we’ve had around food, just to name a few. Good nutrition is being aware of your needs through education, and being aware of your hunger cues and emotions related to eating. A healthy relationship and understanding of food can help with creating balance in nutritional intake.

From the many people I’ve met, it’s clear that nutrition has become very confusing to many. One of my ideas is to create a way to counsel clients on nutrition, and then set up or create a personalized app function for the client to use (and for RDs to use to serve clients) to become more independent in their journey for balanced nutrition.
